Italian Language Practice Session 1 – Study Notes
These notes summarize the key language points, vocabulary, and cultural themes from your new Italian conversation transcript. You can copy this content into a document editor (like Google Docs or Word) and export it as a PDF for your Replit app.

1. Introductions & Adjectives
Practice introducing yourself:
Mi chiamo [Name] — My name is [Name]

Use an adjective starting with the same letter as your name to describe yourself.

Example: Mi chiamo Alessia. Sono Allegra.
(My name is Alessia. I am cheerful.)

Other examples: Giovanna (giovane – young), Giuliana (gioiosa – joyful), Jesse (grande – big).

2. Places & Origins
Talk about where you are from or where you live:

Vengo da... — I come from...

Abito a... — I live in...

Example places from the lesson: Palermo, Londra (London), Brighton, Germania (Germany), Napoli (Naples), Roma (Rome).

3. Weather & Time
Describing the weather:

caldo — hot

nuvoloso — cloudy

piovoso — rainy

Discussing days and routines:

Days of the week (e.g., sabato – Saturday, domenica – Sunday)

Times of day (e.g., la mattina – morning, la sera – evening)

Common questions:

Com’era il tempo? — How was the weather?

A che ora...? — At what time...?

4. Shopping & Food Vocabulary
Useful verbs:

comprare — to buy

mangiare — to eat

cucinare — to cook

Example foods and phrases:

Vorrei... — I would like...

Quanto costa? — How much does it cost?

Posso assaggiare? — Can I taste?

Una fetta di... — A slice of...

Un etto/mezzo chilo/un chilo — 100g/half a kilo/a kilo

5. Grammar Points
Reflexive verbs:

alzarsi — to get up

chiamarsi — to call oneself (used for introductions)

Direct/Indirect Object Pronouns:

lo (him/it), la (her/it), li (them, masc.), le (them, fem.), ne (of it/them, for quantities)

Examples:

Mi chiamo Alessia. — My name is Alessia.

Ne vorrei due. — I’d like two (of them).

6. Activities & Hobbies
Talking about hobbies and routines:

camminare — to walk

giardinaggio — gardening

fare yoga — to do yoga

zumba — Zumba

Expressing feelings:

felice — happy

entusiasta — enthusiastic

energico — energetic

7. Cultural Notes
Italian social life:

Language meetups, conversation groups, café gatherings.

Italian food culture: potluck dinners, bringing dishes to share, socializing at cafés.

Expressions for feelings and experiences:

Mi fa stare bene. — It makes me feel good.

Mi rende felice. — It makes me happy.

Mi scarica. — It helps me unwind.

9. Practice Prompts
Introduce yourself and describe yourself with an adjective that starts with your name’s letter.

Ask and answer questions about where you are from and where you live.

Describe the weather on a recent day.

Role-play shopping at a market: order food, ask prices, and request quantities.

Share your favorite activities and how they make you feel.
